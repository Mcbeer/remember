# Remember

A family-oriented shared todo application. Members keep personal lists and
participate in shared family lists, with the future ability to be reminded
about items via Web Push before they are due.

## Language

**User**:
An authenticated person, identified by an OAuth login (Google/GitHub/etc.).
_Avoid_: account, member, person

**List**:
A named collection of Items. A List is either personal (owned by one User) or
shared among the Users of a Family.
_Avoid_: todo, board

**Item**:
A single thing to be done, belonging to exactly one List. Has an optional due
time and a completed/not-completed state. For things that repeat, use a Schedule
instead. An Item has an origin (created by a User, or suggested by Email
Ingestion); a suggested Item is _pending_ until a Member confirms it.
_Avoid_: todo, task, entry

**Schedule**:
A recurrence rule (e.g. "17:30 every Tuesday") belonging to a List, describing a
thing that repeats. A Schedule generates Occurrences; it is never "completed".
_Avoid_: event, recurring item, series

**Occurrence**:
A single dated instance of a Schedule (e.g. this Tuesday's 17:30). An Occurrence
is not an Item; it carries its own completed/skipped state. Occurrences are
computed from the Schedule's rule and only persisted when they have such state or
a one-off edit (an exception).
_Avoid_: instance, event, item

**Family**:
A group of Users who share one or more Lists. A User may belong to multiple
Families.
_Avoid_: group, team, household, organization

**Membership**:
The link between a User and a Family, granting equal access to all of that
Family's shared Lists. All Memberships are equal — there are no roles.
_Avoid_: role, participant

**Invite**:
A reusable, expiring link/code generated by a Member that lets another User join
the Family. Regenerating it invalidates the old one.
_Avoid_: token, code (as a standalone term)

**Email Ingestion** _(future / out of scope for v1)_:
An inbound email, sent to a List's or Family's unique secret address, that a
small LLM reads to suggest one or more Items (or a Schedule). The unique address
both routes the email to the right List and acts as its authorisation. Suggested
Items are _pending_ until a Member confirms them.
_Avoid_: import, parser, bot

**Reminder** _(future / out of scope for v1)_:
A scheduled Web Push notification fired a configured amount of time before a due
moment — either an Item's due time or a Schedule's next Occurrence. An Item or a
Schedule may have multiple Reminders.
_Avoid_: notification, alert, alarm

## Relationships

- A **User** owns zero or more personal **Lists**
- A **User** has zero or more **Memberships**, each to one **Family**
- A **Family** has one or more **Memberships** (one per **User**)
- A **Family** has one or more shared **Lists**
- A **List** contains zero or more **Items** and zero or more **Schedules**
- An **Item** belongs to exactly one **List**
- A **Schedule** belongs to exactly one **List** and generates zero or more **Occurrences**
- An **Occurrence** belongs to exactly one **Schedule**
- An **Item** or a **Schedule** has zero or more **Reminders** _(future)_

A **List**'s owner is the only sharing axis: a List is visible to exactly the
User who owns it, or to all Members of the Family that owns it. There is no
ad-hoc sharing of an individual List with specific Users.

When the last Member leaves a Family, the Family and its shared Lists and Items
are deleted. **Items** belong to a **List**, never to the **User** who created
them, so a leaving Member's Items remain while the Family still has Members.

## Example dialogue

> **Dev:** "When a User joins a Family, do they get a copy of its Lists?"
> **Domain expert:** "No — the Lists belong to the Family. The Membership just
> grants the User access to the Family's shared Lists. There's no copy."

> **Dev:** "Gymnastics is every Tuesday at 17:30. Is that an Item?"
> **Domain expert:** "No — that's a Schedule. It generates an Occurrence each
> Tuesday. An Item is a single thing you complete; a Schedule never gets
> completed. When someone ticks off this Tuesday's gymnastics, that's the
> Occurrence being marked done — next Tuesday's Occurrence is untouched."

> **Dev:** "If we cancel just next week's gymnastics, does the rule change?"
> **Domain expert:** "No — the Schedule's rule stays. You skip that one
> Occurrence. We only store an Occurrence when it has its own state, like being
> skipped or completed, or a one-off edit."

> **Dev:** "The school emails homework in. Does that create an Item straight
> away?"
> **Domain expert:** "It creates a suggested Item — pending — from Email
> Ingestion. A Member has to confirm it before it counts. We never let the
> machine write a real Item unreviewed."

## Flagged ambiguities

- "list" was used for both personal and shared collections — resolved: both are
  **Lists**; the distinction is whether the List is owned by a **User** or a
  **Family**.
